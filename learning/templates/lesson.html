Lesson 1: Solar Basics

Solar energy is derived from sunlight and is one of the most abundant renewable resources available. Kenya, with its year-round sunshine, is uniquely positioned to lead in solar power adoption and innovation.

Solar energy can be harnessed using photovoltaic (PV) panels that convert sunlight into electricity. This clean, renewable energy reduces dependence on fossil fuels, lowers utility bills, and contributes to environmental protection.

Key Concepts

Solar Energy

Energy produced from sunlight, converted into electricity or heat through solar technologies.

Solar Panels

Devices made up of photovoltaic cells that directly convert sunlight into electricity.

Why Kenya?
  • Consistent 4-6 kWh/m² solar radiation daily.
  • Ideal for off-grid homes, schools, and clinics.
  • Supports Vision 2030 sustainable energy goals.

Lesson 2: Energy Storage

Solar energy is not always available when needed. Energy storage systems allow excess solar power generated during the day to be stored for use at night or during cloudy periods.

Efficient storage is essential for reliable power supply in off-grid homes and businesses, ensuring that clean energy remains accessible 24/7.

Common Storage Technologies
  • Lead-acid Batteries: Affordable, widely used in small systems.
  • Lithium-ion Batteries: Longer lifespan, more compact, ideal for modern homes and businesses.

With proper storage, households can power essential devices like lights, phone chargers, and TVs even when the sun isn’t shining.

Lesson 3: Home Solar Systems

Home solar systems provide an independent, renewable source of power, especially in rural or remote areas with limited grid access.

These systems can be designed to meet basic lighting needs or scaled to power entire households including fridges, TVs, internet routers, and security systems.

Typical Components
  • Solar Panels: Capture sunlight and convert it to electricity.
  • Battery Bank: Stores excess power for later use.
  • Inverter: Converts DC (from panels) to AC power for home use.
  • Charge Controller: Regulates charging to protect the batteries.

Solar systems empower homes to reduce reliance on costly grid power and improve energy security sustainably.
